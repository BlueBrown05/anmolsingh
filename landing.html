Oracle NetSuite

This is a personal project for my portfolio not a commissioned one.

What I did in this project?

In this project, I have re-imagined the look and feel of NetSuite’s Login, Company SSO, Expenses & Timer Screen.

What is an ERP & benefit of ERP system like NetSuite in India?

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) are systems that are one of the most crucial assets for any organization because it tries to integrate all the different departments and functions of an organization into a single computer system to serve the various needs of these departments. This not only gives us a birds eye view of the needs of the various departments of the organization. But also this integrated approach can be very cost-effective for an organization; with an integrated solution, different departments can easily share information and communicate with one another.

And seeing the current scenario where the pandemic has forced the business to carry out their work online, Oracle NetSuite can become an important asset which the small and rising businesses need; Especially in developing countries like India. In India where an individual can easily afford an android smartphone than a desktop, it’s easier for the Oracle NetSuite Android App to create a market for itself through Google Playstore due to the large availability of android users. Hence if we can rethink the android application from a businessperson’s perspective it will be very beneficial for rising businesses.

Why did I choose this project?

Apart from the opportunity mentioned in the previous paragraph which is one of the key reasons I opted for this project there are a few more reasons which motivated me to consider doing it and they, are as follows:

  • The scale of impact of this project/application is humongous.
  • It’ll make more than thousands of users’ lives easier.
  • There is a gaping hole to fix between the desktop application and Mobile Application, especially for android.
  • Users of this application have been asking for it. Source: Android Play store.
  • Opportunity to work on a real-world application.

My Goal

My goal is the applied version of my motivation for opting for this case study. Which is to rethink the application’s UI/UX from scratch keeping in mind,

  • To preserve the functionality of its old version.
  • Application’s Accessibility & Usability.

The redesign process


For the redesign, I selected one screen or goal at a time and followed the process below —

  1. UX Research — First, I identified key pain points in each screen and the user flows from one current screen to another.
  2. Ideate — Next, I came up with ideas to overcome the pain points and redesigned one screen at a time
  3. Design — I designed each screen and user flow. (Design — Adobe XD, Prototype — Protopie)
  4. Test — I conducted two rounds of user testing with 5 of my friends and improved the design from their feedback.

Research- finding flaws

I approached researching for this project with 3 research methods in mind User one on one Interviews, Accessibility testing and Google Play store reviews.

But due to some unexpected events and busy schedule of business persons I couldn’t get their one-on-one interview so I had to move ahead and go for Play store reviews directly. On Playstore, I decided to go for design only reviews which were low rated and highly rated as well to learn what made the user give that kind of review.

After reviewing I did an Accessibility test as well to get the perspective of users with diverse abilities. For this, I used the Google Accessibility Scanner app. And from the results, it was vivid that, how much this application was in dire need of rethinking.

Lastly, I used my own personal insights to try to get into the shoes of a user who is using the app for the first time.

Google Play store reviews

Google play store reviews showed that there was little to no satisfaction with the current UI and how much users wanted a new user interface and experience.

Furthermore, from several YouTube videos, I gathered that NetSuite has a steep learning curve which is understandable from the fact that how feature-rich NetSuite app is, and I also saw that this learning curve is being flattened by several NetSuite videos so, hence we just needed to make it accessible from inside the application.

All in all after knowing the audience reaction to the current version, I gained lots of knowledge on what to fix but, still it wasn't enough to locate the problem due to its generalized nature; Hence, I used the app on my own and tried to locate all the pain points for a user logging in for the first time.

Personal thoughts- We really needed to think in terms of customer service and how can this application help users when they are stuck at some point from inside the application.
